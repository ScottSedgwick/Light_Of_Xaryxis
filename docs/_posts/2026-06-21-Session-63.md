# Give the gods some privacy

Bahamut and Lakal have a moment.  It's super awkward. Get a room, guys!

The Quom are looking all contrite now, but they're in trouble.  Lakal gives them an earful - except for Tholis, who is cool.

Bahamut has a chat to Rusty, and says his gratitude is boundless.  So infinitely boundless that he gives us 4 consumable items.  Woohoo!

<details><summary>Bahamut's gifts</summary>
<ul>
  <li><b>Scale of the Platinum Dragon.</b> Crafted from one of Bahamut's own shed scales. When consumed, it grants the player the form and abilities of an adult silver dragon for one day.</li>
  <li><b>3 Platinum Beads.</b> Anyone in the party can negate any damage they take in a single instance. 1 bead consumed each time.</li>
</ul>
</details>

He also gives Rusty his socials so they can catch up later for drinks.

# Onward into the confusing Astral

We find 2 doors, floating in the Astral, with two statues next to them.  They attempt to mock the party, but they're pretty stupid, and we ignore them.  They don't take very kindly to being mocked back, either.  None the less, the party is experienced enough to recognise a plot hook when they see one, so we approach them.

They begin their spiel: "One of us always tells the truth..."

Rusty finishes for them: "and the other one always lies, right?"

"Have you heard this one?"

"Yep."  Rusty approaches the female statue, and says "I'll talk to you, because you're prettier." We learn that statues can blush - that's something Rusty didn't know.  It's never a wasted day when you learn something new.  "If I were to ask the other statue which door led to danger, which one would he say?" She points at the left door, and we go through the right one.  As we depart, we hear them saying "It's been eons since anyone got that right", which is a sad indictment on the quality of adventurers these days.

While we're on the subject of learning something new, we discovered another colour for Astral Gates.  Green gates lead to the Beastlands, apparently.  Never heard of that plane.  We should visit.

# The domain of dead gods

We do a bunch of History checks and get a lore dump.  Read the Notes.  It boils down to: Leira was the Goddess of Deception until Bhaal ganked her and stole her domain.  When Bhaal got ganked, it passed to Cyric, but passed back to Bhaal when he was resurrected.  If we find Leira's shards of divinity and return them to her, she can recover her domain.

Frankly, we don't trust her.  However, if we help her, Bhaal suffers?  Now that is a noble cause!  We're on the case!

She offers us the following rewards for helping her:

<details><summary>Leira's rewards</summary>
Each character will receive one favour:
<ul>
  <li>The answer to any question, guaranteed truthful</li>
  <li>An illusion that can deceive even divine sight, lasting 1d4 days after activating.</li>
  <li>The ability to detect any lie told in their presence for 1d6 days after activating.</li>
  <li>Safe passage through any deception-based challenge they will face in the future</li>
  <li>Knowledge of a secret that could change the course of their quest</li>
</ul>
</details>

First problem: divine shards burn.  Neither Rusty nor Louie are close enough (yet) to divine ascension to be able to safely handle them.  However, the construction of a gyroscope to contain a shard is a design that is common(ish) knowledge amongst worshippers of Haephestus/Porphyrous - which Rusty is!  He needs enough raw materials to construct 3, and the raw materials cost 2000 GP each.  Also, we need to find somewhere to buy them.

# The return of Danny DeTiefling

As soon as the party thinks of the need to buy something, the doors open again.  The voices of the statues can be heard: "What are the odds?  Eons, then 2 in one day?"  

Entering is a short, wide Tiefling.  Rusty recognises Techies.  Louie doesn't.  Ethan forgot the game Techies usually offers, and makes the mistake of offering Baldur's Bones, which is effectively Blackjack with dice.  He _also_ foolishly allows Scott to insert a house variant allowing an automatic win after a set number of hits - and he also foolishly sets that limit as 5.  The average of 5d6 is 18, which means automatic win for the player most of the time.  That house rule was rescinded very quickly once play began.

Other rule clarifications - ties roll over, there is no "dealer wins on tie" rule.  The dealer is not constrained to a soft limit of 17.  However, using inspiration for a re-roll _is_ allowed, which means we win every game.

Ethan did remember Techies original game eventually - 2d6, win on a 7 or 12 - and allowed us the option of switching to that.  Louie briefly entertained the idea, until Rusty told him the odds.  Gambling is a tax on people who can't do math.

The end result was +4 max HP for Rusty, and +2 for Louie, I think.  We sold some stuff, and got pretty good money for it.  We bought the materials we wanted.  We made a pretty good profit overall.

Rusty opens up his mansion and whips up 3 gyroscopes with a magnificent crafting roll. +1 Piety.

# Next puzzle please

For some reason, every puzzle involves a door.  This door has a face.  It is overjoyed to see Louie, because it's had an itchy nose for millennia, and can't scratch it. Louie touches it with his poison finger and it's very happy.

It wants us to tell it something provably false to continue.  Rusty goes for the surreal option first: "This sentence is a lie."  The door thinks for a bit, then says "No paradoxes!"

So we go with "Your nose has never been itchy."  The door opens - it's mouth, which is pitch black inside.  We've seen this before - Rusty tests the blackness by poking his 10' pole 5' in, then pulling it back.  When Rusty is now _not_ the owner of a 5' pole, we go through.

# The mirrors of Gnihtyreve

We find ourselves at one end of a hallway lined with mirrors, reflecting us, but slightly different.  At the far end (~1000 feet away) we can see a pair of crystal drama masks.  Everyone make Wisdom saves.  Everyone _except_ Raymond passes - Raymond is mesmerised by a mirror, showing a reflection of him riding Louie.  Weird kink, but I'm not going to shame.  Louie distracts him with a Scooby Snack, and now what we are up against is clear.

Wisdom saves again!  This time only Rusty fails, but it is apparent that the DC is dangerously high, and we have a long way to go.  Wit re-centers Rusty with judicious use of Charm Person.  

We make it the rest of the way by: Wit casting Darkness, centered on himself, so the entire party - except Wit - are blinded.  He then grabs hold of everyone and leads them down the hall.  Louie has an aura going that gives Wit +5 on saves.  Rusty continuously casts Resistance on Wit all the way - Resistance requires touch, not to be able to see the target.  That's another +1d4.  Wit's OK at Wis saves, too - +7.  We make it to the other end, only to run into something that behaves exactly like a Wall of Force, but which Ethan _explicitly_ states is **not** a Wall of Force - Wit was winding up with disintegrate.

We have to find the _one_ mirror in the hall (there's a different one for each character) that reflects each of us exactly.  Rather than spend an inordinate amount of time searching, and saving, Ethan rolls 1d100 and gives us that many seconds (57) to run and find an actual mirror in our house.  37 seconds later and Zoe and Scott are both back with different, _large_ mirrors.  None of this namby-pamby hand mirrors for us, no sir!

We reach the mask, and in our attempt to encase it in a gyroscope, it leaps off its pedestal, and attaches itself to Rusty's face.  Rusty glimpses the universe through the domain of lies, and has to save against - something.  He made his save.  We encase the shard.

This shard allows the holder to cast Mislead once per day.

# Another door appears!

This one has 3 "seals".  They're really just fancy buttons. They have labels!

| Seal | Label |
| ---- | ----- |
| A | B is lying |
| B | C is lying | 
| C | A and B are lying |

Ethan expects significant effort to go into this.  Rusty presses Seal B almost immediately.  Ethan is pissed off that he guessed right, but Rusty explains he didn't guess - he threw together a truth table, and once you made the mental leap that C's statement could be false if A _or_ B were telling the truth (rather than needing both of them to be telling the truth) then the only way for it to work is if B was telling the truth.  Too easy.

We spent quite a while trying to get Ethan to specify how the statues were arranged, because their positioning was relevant to solving any puzzle.  The fact that Ethan had not worked out where they were in advance was one clue, as was:

The floor of the hall still looked suspicious - it was cobblestones that just **screamed** traps - so Rusty moved along the hall, tapping with his 10' pole that he still hadn't put away.

We ignored the statues entirely and just opened the unlocked door.

# The Library of Alexandria

We find ourselves in a _spectacular_ library, but at a closer glance, the books are all blank.  Still, what a collection of notebooks!  They whisper messages to us all:

<details><summary>What the books said</summary>
<ul>
  <li>The betrayer stands among you, waiting for the perfect moment...</li>
  <li>The goddess lies - she was never slain, only sleeping...</li>
  <li>Your greatest enemy knows you are here...</li>
  <li>One of you will not leave this place alive...</li>
  <li>The fragment is cursed - taking it means dooming your world...</li>
  <li>Leira serves Bhaal now, has always served Bhaal...</li>
  <li>This is all an illusion - you never left the Material Plane...</li>
  <li>The exit disappeared when you entered...</li>
  <li>You have been here for years, though it feels like minutes...</li>
  <li>Your memories are false, planted by the goddess...</li>
</ul>
</details>

Sounds like bullshit to us.  We ignore them.

At the centre of the library, there is a large book inscribed with glyphs (it's another shard, pretty obviously).  As we approach, we find a statue of Ao interposing itself between us and the book.

We have to tell it 3 things about ourselves - two truths, and one lie.  If Ao can correctly guess the lie, we cannot pass.

<details><summary>Rusty's Statements</summary>
<ol>
  <li>Rusty did his journeyman project on a machine to allow Flumphs to fart.</li>
  <li>Rusty stole a brain from a spaceship</li>
  <li>Rusty used the Alter Self spell to grow legs to see how walking works, so he could build his armour.</li>
</ol>
</details>

Ao then asked clarifying questions, which wasn't in the rules, but what can you do?  We answered them, and he still got it wrong by guessing number 1.  Rusty did, in fact, make a machine to allow Flumphs to fart, _and he still has it!_  Rusty's lie was number 3.

<details><summary>Louie's Statements</summary>
<ul>
  <li>Louie is still hunting the shapeshifter.</li>
  <li>Louie still thinks Dennis turning into a Great Dane is one of the funniest things he has ever seen.</li>
  <li>Louie still has feeblemind moss.</li>
</ul>
</details>

Ao once again asked for clarification - to be fair, Dennis didn't remember being turned into a dog, but Louie regaled us with the story of the time Dennis got into a drinking competition in The Spooky Place, the bar in Revalia.  Our memories were refreshed, and we all had a chuckle again.

Ao guessed (largely optimistically) the the lie was the Louie still had Feeblemind moss.  Unfortunately for Ethan, Louie _does_ still have moss left.  The lie was turning into a Great Dane.  Dennis was explicitly turned into a _Burmese Mountain Dog_.

Companions had different rules - they just made one statement, and Ao guessed if it was true or false.

Wit said: "I have 3 different ways to Teleport", which was a bit of a trick question.  Ao said it was false, and got it wrong - but he would have got it wrong if he said true too.  Wit has **4** different ways to Teleport - Blink, Misty Step, Dimension Door and Teleportation Circle.

Raymond said: "Raymond, at all times, has at least 3 swords in his mouth".  Ao correctly guessed that this was false, but for the wrong reasons.  Raymond does carry 3 swords in his mouth - but while fighting, only two are technically _in_ his mouth.

Raymonds second try was: "Even if Raymond were not immune to poison, he would still be immune to poison."  Ao, thoroughly confused by that, said it was a lie.  Nope.  Raymond is naturally immune to poison, as a gift from Ao.  He also wears a fancy hat that _also_ makes him immune to poison.  So, by Ethans rules, poison damage heals Raymond. Makes sense to us.

# Pile o'Treasure

The last barrier between us and the book is a literal _mountain_ of treasure.  We assumed it was all illusory, and also that it would do damage if we touched it.  We wanted to fly over it, but apparently all methods of flight suddenly stopped working.  This was problematic, because Wit has no movement rate that _isn't_ flying.  So Ethan allowed Wit - and only Wit - to hover one foot above the ground.

The treasure mountain had no path through it - you had to climb over it.  Given that there was no way not to touch it, we just started stomping through.  We left behind us the gold pieces and myriad magic items.  After the fact, Ethan revealed to us that all the items were real, but would disappear after 24 hours.  However, if we took the treasure and then used one of the wishes from the Ring of Three Wishes that was in the pile to wish for the treasure to last forever, it would have worked.  Oh well.

We get to the book, which despite the appearance it took in the art Ethan showed us, is apparently a Quill.  To extract the Quill, we need to correctly answer 5 riddles.  They're not _really_ riddles, except for the last one, which was pretty good.
