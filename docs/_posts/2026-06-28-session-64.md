# Horus and Cletus' big adventure

The party explored the Astral Plane, passing a tabaxi rogue, an ogre, and even a talking mule. Before long, they encountered three kobold children stacked in a trench coat, demanding candy. The party obliged.

Not far ahead, they found a doll of Cletus, clearly a taunt from his Uncle Krampus. Soon after, they discovered a diamond worth 10,000 gold, which revealed itself to be a giant mimic. It badly injured Horus before the party managed to banish it.

In need of rest, they came across a wizard in a tower who beckoned them inside. Without hesitation, they accepted. He fed them and explained he was a fire wizard working on amplifying fire spells. He joked that he could have been a fae while they ate his food, and casually mentioned the danger of sharing true names, something that had once gotten him into trouble. The party slept while he continued his research.

When they woke, the wizard was dead, reduced to a skeleton at his workbench. His notes revealed he had been experimenting with increasing the amount of bat guano in his spells. He believed adding a kilo would amplify the magic. It did, but it also caused him to combust. Horus recovered an intact page from the ruined spellbook, allowing him to learn a spell of up to 5th level from any class.

As they left, the tower began to fade, its summoner now dead. Outside, they found a dying Githyanki. As they tried to help, three red-armoured Githyanki confronted them, accusing them of causing it. Before the situation could be resolved, a dozen floating bags drifted in around them. In a horrifying moment, all three Githyanki were dragged into the bags.
Krampus’s voice echoed as the party realised they were facing bagmen emerging from Bags of Devouring. The creatures tried to drag them inside, succeeding several times. Eventually, Horus was devoured, and Cletus could not save him in time.

Cletus, assuming Horus would return somehow due to his time-travelling nature, pressed on. Meanwhile, Horus discovered that anyone consumed by a Bag of Devouring becomes a bagman. To reverse the transformation, he needed to drag three others into bags in his place.

He searched through countless Bags of Devouring within the void. One victim was a quom tied to an old regime. Another appeared to be an abuser about to murder their partner, though it turned out to be a staged performance, which left Horus unsettled. The third was a tabaxi rogue caught stealing. Once the task was complete, Horus returned to normal and emerged from one of the bags Cletus had kept.

Reunited and in need of relief, the two stumbled upon a hag who used urine to fertilise her crops. Anyone who consumed the crops would experience the memories of whoever provided the fertiliser. Completely unconcerned, they made a trade, giving their urine in exchange for proficiency in a skill of their choice.

Continuing on, they discovered a frozen battlefield. Horus recognised a device called the Still Heart, which had frozen time across the conflict. Using chronomancy, they restored it, allowing the battle between Githyanki, fiends, celestials, and other warriors to resume. They left the chaos behind.
They later found a frozen carol choir, another taunt from Krampus. Soon after, Krampus himself ambushed them, delivering an angry monologue about how they had wronged him and caused his banishment to the Astral Plane. Seeking revenge, he tried to intimidate them.

Instead, Horus and Cletus pretended not to recognise him. They played dumb so convincingly that it completely threw him off, demoralising him and undermining his entire rant. Despite his attempts to remind them of their shared past, they continued the act until he lost composure.

Taking advantage, they defeated him and shoved him into his own Bag of Devouring, killing Saint Nick’s brother and Cletus’s uncle. All that remained was a Cape of the Mountebank, which Cletus claimed.

Shortly after, Horus received a message from Wit. Louis and Rusty had completed their distraction at Lira’s lair. Horus then bent space and time, returning the group to the rune they had placed outside it.
